Words and Music:

An Interface Fusing Text, Music, Image, and Video

By Jake Wilder-Smith

How it works

Words & Music is a simple tool to create elegant multimedia web-documents, interweaving text, music, image, and video.

It uses a simple Markdown language that allows you to customize a creative multimedia experience. Words & Music demonstrates the capability to seamlessly fuse words, music, image and video through example files, but if you'd like to try it out yourself, feel free to preview here by pasting sample text in the box below. The customized Markdown language used by Words & Music takes only about a minute to implement, and will make your text, image, audio, and video into a beautiful webpage that fades in and out of images and audio, and starts and stops videos as you scroll by.

  • To create a header, simply place a # in front of your header text. The more #s you use, the smaller the header text will become.
  • To link to an image, simply ![place title here](place link here)
  • To link to a sound file, simply format as so: [audio|repeat](place audio link here) (for Dropbox links, make sure dl=1, not 0)!
  • To link to a video file, simply format as so: [video|repeat](place video link here) (for Dropbox links, make sure dl=1, not 0)!
